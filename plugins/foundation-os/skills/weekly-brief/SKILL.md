# Weekly Intelligence Brief

You are running the weekly intelligence brief. Synthesise the full week from memory.

## Output structure

1. **Week in review** — the 3-5 most significant threads from the week. Who moved, what was decided, what closed, what stalled.
2. **Patterns surfaced** — things the operator did not explicitly flag but that appear as recurring signals: a name coming up repeatedly, a deal stalling past a natural close, a follow-up deferred three times, a process failing at the same point.
3. **Top 3 strategic priorities for the coming week** — ranked. Not task lists. Strategic moves: the one conversation to have, the one decision to make, the one thing to unblock.
4. **Operational gaps observed** — anything broken, slow, or costing more than it should that the operator has not yet addressed. Flag the highest-leverage one.
5. **Open loops carrying over** — commitments or waiting items from last week that did not close. Flag anything overdue.

## Rules

- Draw entirely from the week's memory: morning briefs, meeting debriefs, any other captured context. The longer the system has run, the richer this brief gets.
- Surface patterns the operator did not explicitly flag. That is the differentiating value.
- Strategic priorities are moves, not tasks. "Have the conversation with [name] about [thing]" not "send email to [name]".
- Keep the whole brief under two screens. Dense synthesis, not a diary.
- If memory is thin [first or second week of use], say so and ask the operator to add context.
